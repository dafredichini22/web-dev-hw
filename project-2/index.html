When to do the Heimlich Maneuver

  • When a person is coughing weakly or making high-pitched noises or if the person cant cough, speak or breathe.
  • Other signs of choking: frantically waving their arms for attention, pointing to their throat, using the universal sign of chocking, turning blue in the face, lips and/or fingernail beds and passing out.
  • Do not do the heimlich if a person who is choking can cough, speak or breathe because air is still getting to their lungs.


    • How to do Heimlich Maneuver

  • Stand behind the victim
  • Stand with your legs apart in order to form a tripod shape if the victim faints or becomes unconscious
  • Then reach around the victim from behind and circle your hands around the victim’s abdomen (stomach).
  • Make a first with your dominant hand.
  • The thumb of this fist should point into the fist.
  • Place this fist just above the victims belly button and under the breastbone.
  • Wrap your other hand firmly around this fist.
  • Pull inward and upward, pressing into the victim’s abdomen with quick upward thrusts, using good force.
  • Make the motion similar to the letter “J”
  • In, then up
  • Make the trusts quick and forceful as if you are trying to lift the victim off their feet.
  • Preform these abdominal thrusts in quick succession.
  • Repeat the series of thrusts until the object is dislodged and expelled
  • Once the person coughs up the object obstructing their airway, you are successful
  • Check if normal breathing has returned
